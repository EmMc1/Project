Check the Weather

Why children should learn about the weather

Teaching children about the weather and climate can contribute to their development and environmental stewardship as they learn about the relationship with changing weather patterns and the effects of global warming.

Building Scientific Curiosity

Introducing children to weather and climate ignites their natural curiosity about the world around them. They will build on the scientific skills of observation, questioning, and foster their scientific thinking and problem-solving skills. This early exposure can lay the foundation for future interests in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

Weather and climate discussions also offer opportunities for critical thinking and analysis. Kids can explore the causes and effects of weather phenomena, helping them develop analytical skills and the ability to assess information critically.

Cross Curricular Ideas

Ideas to try out in your classroom.

Maths

  • Find differences between temperatures at given times eg 3am and 9pm.
  • Find the difference between temperatures in different countries around the world.
  • Find the time difference between the highest temperature and the lowest temperature.
  • Graph the weather of an area on a chart using coordinates.
  • If the temperature got higher/lower by 5 degrees, what would the new temperature be?

Literacy

  • Create weather forecasts based on the weather in which the children will plan, write and edit a script to be performed. The children could film their weather segments also.
  • Create a comparison chart about different countries' weather picking out similarities and differences.
  • Create a poem based on the weather in an area.
  • Use adjectives to describe the weather at different times.

Social, Environmental and Scientific Education

  • Research the temperature variations in different countries e.g. Australia Sydney and Perth and explore why.
  • Create a project on a given country.
  • Explore how weather data is collected.
  • Explore the effects of climate change on areas and how they are affecting people who live there.
  • Compare weather data that they have researched to the weather in Ireland.
